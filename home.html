Brief Introduction

I was born in Athens, Greece, in December 1992 where I spent my childhood, teenage and early adulthood. Inspired by my grandfather, a naval engineer with a great experience on engines, I decided quite early to study mechanical engineering. In 2010 I was accepted in the National Technical University of Athens (NTUA) for a five-year study program from which I acquired my diploma in 2015. During those years I discovered my passion for numerical simulation, and I had the opportunity to work on aerodynamic and aeroelastic codes for simulating wind turbines loads (here you may find the resulting publications).

I wanted to specialize in the Finite Element Method (FEM), so In 2016 I moved to Switzerland to attend a Masters program in the Swiss Federal Institute of Technology (ETHZ) with focus on FEM, structural optimization and numerical modeling of composite materials. I studied the crack initiation and propagation in composite plates by programming a specialized finite element software. In 2018 I was awarded ETH’s silver medal for my work.

Since my graduation in 2017 I have worked in several industrial sectors. My work always revolves around structural and thermal simulations. In the section Industry experience, I have provided detailed information on the projects and the responsibilities I handled in each position.
